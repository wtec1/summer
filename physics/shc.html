ğŸ§ª Experiment 4.3: Comparing Specific Heat Capacities

Aim: To compare specific heat capacities of substances.

Apparatus: Thermometers, boiling tubes, water, cooking oil, hot water bath

Procedure:

  • Heat equal volumes of water and oil for same time.
  • Measure temperature rise.

Observation: Oil heats faster than water.

Conclusion: Water has a higher specific heat capacity.

âš™ï¸ Applications of Specific Heat Capacity

  • Water is used in car radiators because it absorbs a lot of heat with little temperature rise.
  • Cooking utensils are made of materials with low specific heat capacity so they heat up quickly.
  • Sensitive thermometers use materials with low specific heat capacity to respond rapidly to temperature changes.
  • Handles of heating devices are made from materials with high specific heat capacity to reduce overheating.
  • Water regulates body temperature because of its high specific heat capacity.
  • Land and sea breezes occur because land heats and cools faster than water.
