Heat transfer is the movement of heat energy from a hotter region to a colder region. Heat can be transferred through solids, liquids and gases.

  • Conduction: Transfer of heat through solids without movement of particles.
  • Convection: Transfer of heat in liquids and gases by movement of particles.
  • Radiation: Transfer of heat through electromagnetic waves; no medium required.

ğŸ”© Rate of Heat Conduction in Metals

Different metals conduct heat at different rates.

  • Good conductors: copper, aluminium
  • Poor conductors: iron, steel

ğŸ  Applications of Heat Transfer

  • Black surfaces absorb heat efficiently (solar collectors).
  • Dark clothes absorb more heat than light clothes.
  • White-painted walls reflect heat.
  • Aluminium foil reduces heat loss in food containers.
  • Solar cookers and heaters use radiation.
  • Thermal flasks and cooler boxes reduce heat transfer.
  • Grass-thatched houses reduce heat gain.
