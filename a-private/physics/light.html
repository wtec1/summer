Light travels in straight lines. This can be shown using experiments such as aligning pinholes or observing shadows formed by opaque objects.

When an object blocks light, a shadow is formed behind it, showing that light does not bend around corners.

Shadows are formed when light from a source is blocked by an opaque object.

  • Umbra: The dark central part of a shadow.
  • Penumbra: The partially dark outer region.

Ray diagrams are used to show how the umbra and penumbra are formed.

ğŸŒ Eclipses

An eclipse occurs when one celestial body blocks light from another.

  • Solar eclipse: The Moon blocks sunlight from reaching the Earth.
  • Lunar eclipse: The Earth blocks sunlight from reaching the Moon.

Ray diagrams are used to explain how both solar and lunar eclipses occur.

ğŸ“· Pinhole Camera

A pinhole camera works on the principle that light travels in straight lines. It forms a real, inverted image on a screen.

Ray diagrams are used to show image formation, and magnification can be calculated using image and object distances.

ğŸª Reflection of Light

Reflection is the bouncing back of light when it strikes a surface such as a plane mirror.

Plane mirrors reflect light regularly and produce clear images.

ğŸ“ Laws of Reflection

  • The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.
  • The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane.

ğŸ–¼ï¸ Images Formed by Plane Mirrors

  • The image is virtual and upright.
  • The image is the same size as the object.
  • The image is laterally inverted.
  • The image is the same distance behind the mirror as the object is in front.
