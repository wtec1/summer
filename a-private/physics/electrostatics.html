üîç Introduction

Electrostatics is the study of electric charges at rest. It explains how charges exist in matter, how objects become charged, and how charged objects interact. Many everyday phenomena such as lightning, electric shocks, and photocopying are explained using electrostatics.

‚öõÔ∏è Existence of Charges in Matter

Matter is made up of atoms. Each atom contains a nucleus with positively charged protons and neutral neutrons, surrounded by negatively charged electrons. An object becomes charged when it gains or loses electrons.

‚ûï‚ûñ Types and Units of Charge

  • There are two types of electric charges: positive and negative.
  • Like charges repel while unlike charges attract.
  • The SI unit of electric charge is the coulomb (C).

‚ö° Charging Objects

Objects can be charged through the transfer of electrons using different methods.

  • Friction: Rubbing materials together transfers electrons.
  • Contact: Touching a charged object transfers charge.
  • Induction: Charging without direct contact.

üß≤ Conductors and Insulators

Conductors allow electrons to move freely, while insulators restrict electron movement. Metals are good conductors, while materials such as plastic and rubber are insulators.

üìè Electrostatic Force

  • The force increases with an increase in the magnitude of charges.
  • The force decreases as the distance between charges increases.

üå©Ô∏è Applications and Dangers

Electrostatics is used in photocopiers, spray painting, and dust removal. However, it can also be dangerous.

Lightning is a large electrostatic discharge. Earthing and lightning conductors are used to safely transfer excess charge to the ground.
