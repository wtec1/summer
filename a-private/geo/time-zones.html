Time zones are regions of Earth that have adopted the same time. There are 24 time zones, each extending 15 degrees longitude, based on Earth's 360-degree rotation in 24 hours.

History:

Developed by a French railway engineer (1827-1915) to standardize travel schedules. In 1884, an international conference in Washington DC officially determined time zones.

Traditional Time-Telling:

  • Checking sun position (sun time/local time)
  • Listening to cock crows before dawn

Time zones are demarcated by vertical lines every 15 degrees longitude. These lines may curve around land masses or islands to avoid confusion, ensuring people in the same region have the same time.
