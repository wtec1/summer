In agriculture, different members of the community perform different activities. These responsibilities shared between men, women, boys, and girls are referred to as gender roles in agricultural production.

Gender roles in agricultural production are the specific activities and responsibilities traditionally or commonly performed by males and females in farming and related activities.

  • roles may differ between men and women
  • roles can change over time
  • roles vary by culture and community

2Ô∏è‚É£ Gender Roles in Agricultural Production

Common roles often performed by women

  • weeding
  • planting and transplanting
  • processing and storing food
  • small livestock care

Common roles often performed by men

  • land preparation
  • ploughing
  • marketing farm produce
  • construction of structures such as kraals

Note: roles can be shared and are not fixed; anyone can perform any agricultural task.

Positive impacts

  • division of labour increases efficiency
  • work is shared among household members
  • production activities are completed faster

Negative impacts

  • overworking one gender (often women)
  • limiting access to resources for some groups
  • reduced productivity when labour is unequal
