üìò 1. Introduction

The Periodic Table arranges elements in rows (periods) and columns (groups) according to their properties.

Elements show patterns (trends) in physical and chemical properties.

These trends depend mainly on the number of outer (valence) electrons.

üîÑ 1.2 General Trends in the Periodic Table

üß© a. Atomic Radius

Distance between nucleus and outermost electron.

  • ‚Ä¢ Increases down a group ‚Üí more electron shells
  • ‚Ä¢ Decreases across a period ‚Üí stronger pull of nucleus

Example:

Lithium < Sodium < Potassium (radius increases down group)

‚ö° b. Ionisation Energy

Energy needed to remove an outer electron.

  • ‚Ä¢ Decreases down a group ‚Üí outer electrons are farther from nucleus
  • ‚Ä¢ Increases across a period ‚Üí atoms hold electrons more tightly

Example:

Lithium > Sodium > Potassium (Li hardest to remove electron)

üß≤ c. Electron Affinity

Measure of how strongly an atom attracts an extra electron.

  • ‚Ä¢ Increases across a period (atoms become smaller and more positive)
  • ‚Ä¢ Decreases down a group (atoms get larger)

‚öñÔ∏è d. Electronegativity

Ability of an atom to attract electrons in a bond.

  • ‚Ä¢ Increases across a period
  • ‚Ä¢ Decreases down a group
