Soil fertility is the ability of soil to supply nutrients, water and support plant growth. Fertile soil produces healthy crops, while infertile soil gives poor yields.

1ï¸âƒ£ Differences Between Fertile and Infertile Soils

Fertile soil has:

  • enough plant nutrients
  • good structure and texture
  • adequate organic matter (humus)
  • good water-holding capacity
  • high biological activity

Infertile soil has:

  • few nutrients
  • poor structure
  • very sandy or very clayey texture
  • low organic matter
  • poor crop performance
