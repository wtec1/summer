üîç Introduction

The respiratory system is a group of organs that enables breathing by allowing oxygen to enter the body and carbon dioxide to be removed. It is essential for energy production and survival.

üß† Components of the Respiratory System

  • Nasal passage: Filters, warms, and moistens air using mucus and cilia.
  • Epiglottis: Prevents food from entering the trachea.
  • Larynx: Voice box; produces sound.
  • Trachea: Windpipe supported by cartilage rings.
  • Bronchi: Two tubes leading air into each lung.
  • Alveoli: Tiny air sacs for gas exchange.
  • Lungs: Organs that contain bronchi and alveoli.
  • Diaphragm: Muscle controlling chest volume.
  • Intercostal muscles: Move the rib cage during breathing.

üîÑ Breathing Mechanism

Inspiration (Inhalation):

  • Diaphragm contracts and moves down
  • Intercostal muscles contract
  • Rib cage moves up and out
  • Air enters the lungs

Expiration (Exhalation):

  • Diaphragm relaxes and moves up
  • Intercostal muscles relax
  • Rib cage moves down and in
  • Air is forced out of the lungs

üìä Lung Capacity and Volumes

  • Residual volume: ~1200 ml

üöë Artificial Ventilation (Resuscitation)

  • Clear the airway
  • Pinch the nose
  • Blow air into the mouth
  • Chest should rise and fall
  • Continue until breathing resumes
