Experiment 2: Density of an Irregular Solid

Objective:

To determine the density of an irregularly shaped object

üìπ Video Experiment 2

Apparatus:

  • Irregular stone or object
  • Beam balance
  • Measuring cylinder
  • Water

Procedure:

  1. Measure and record the mass (m) of the stone using the beam balance
  2. Fill the measuring cylinder with water to a known level
  3. Record the initial water volume (V‚ÇÅ)
  4. Carefully submerge the stone completely in the water
  5. Record the new water volume (V‚ÇÇ)
  6. Calculate the volume of the stone: V = V‚ÇÇ - V‚ÇÅ
  7. Calculate density: œÅ = m/V

Key Principle:

The displacement method - the volume of water displaced equals the volume of the object

  • Beam balance
  • Metre rule or ruler

Procedure:

  1. Measure the mass (m) of the solid using the beam balance

Objective:

To determine the density of a liquid sample

  • Measuring cylinder
  • Liquid sample (e.g., water, oil)
  • Beam balance

Procedure:

  1. Measure the mass (m‚ÇÅ) of the empty measuring cylinder
  2. Pour a known volume (V) of the liquid into the cylinder

Important:
