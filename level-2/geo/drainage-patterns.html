Drainage pattern is the system of distributing and receiving water by rivers and their tributaries.

Dendritic Pattern

Resembles tree branches; rivers join at angles less than 90¬∞; develops on rocks with equal resistance

Trellis Pattern

Rectangular pattern where rivers join at right angles; alternating hard and soft rocks

Radial Pattern

Rivers flow outward from central highland like a bicycle wheel

Intermittent/Disappearing

Rivers disappear into porous soil or marshes and reappear later

2Ô∏è‚É£ What is a River?

A river is surface runoff confined in natural channels called valleys.

Key Terms:

  • Source: Where a river starts (highlands or lowlands)
  • Watershed/Divide: Highland forming river sources and separating systems
  • River Basin: Land drained by the river system
  • Confluence: Point where two rivers join
  • Tributary: Stream joining a main river

3Ô∏è‚É£ Courses of a River

Upper Course (Youthful Stage)

Process: Vertical erosion

Features: V-shaped valleys, potholes, gorges, rapids, waterfalls, plunge pools, interlocking spurs

Middle Course (Mature Stage)

Process: Lateral erosion

Features: U-shaped valleys, meanders (river bends), cliffs

Lower Course (Old Stage)

Process: Deposition

Features: Flood plains, ox-bow lakes, levees, deltas

4Ô∏è‚É£ Important River Features

Waterfall

Sharp break causing water to plunge from heights

Meander

River bend in middle course

Ox-bow Lake

Lake formed when meander is cut off by deposition

Delta

Triangular deposit at river mouth from sediment

Flood Plain

Low-lying fertile land from deposited sediments

Levee

Raised river banks from sediment deposits

5Ô∏è‚É£ Economic Importance of Rivers

  • Waterfalls can be harnessed for hydroelectric power
  • Deltas and flood plains support fertile agricultural lands
  • Lower course rivers support water transport
  • Rivers provide a source of fish
  • Water for domestic and industrial use

6Ô∏è‚É£ Coastal Landforms

Coasts are narrow strips bordering lakes or seas.

Depositional Landforms

Beaches, sandbars, spits, lagoons, estuaries

Erosional Landforms

Headlands, capes, bays, peninsulas, islands
