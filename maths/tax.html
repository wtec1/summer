üìö Understanding Taxation

Taxation is a crucial life skill that helps us understand how much we pay or receive when buying goods, earning income, or receiving discounts. Tax is usually expressed as a percentage of the original amount.

Key Formulas:

  • Tax Amount: Original Price √ó (Tax Rate √∑ 100)
  • Final Price (with tax): Original Price + Tax Amount
  • Discount Amount: Original Price √ó (Discount Rate √∑ 100)
  • Sale Price: Original Price - Discount Amount
  • Net Amount (after tax): Gross Amount - Tax Amount

Quick Tips:

  • VAT (Value Added Tax) is commonly 16.5% in Malawi
  • Discount reduces the price; tax increases it
  • Always express percentages as decimals when calculating
  • Net means "after deductions" while gross means "before deductions"

Example 1: Calculate VAT

A laptop costs K250,000. If VAT is charged at 16.5%, calculate:

(a) The VAT amount

(b) The total price including VAT

Solution:

(a) VAT Amount:

VAT = 250,000 √ó (16.5 √∑ 100)

VAT = 250,000 √ó 0.165

VAT = K41,250

(b) Total Price:

Total = Original Price + VAT

Total = 250,000 + 41,250

Total = K291,250

Answer: (a) K41,250 (b) K291,250

Example 2: Calculate Discount

A pair of shoes originally costs K18,000. The shop offers a 25% discount. Calculate:

(a) The discount amount

(b) The sale price

Solution:

(a) Discount Amount:

Discount = 18,000 √ó (25 √∑ 100)

Discount = 18,000 √ó 0.25

Discount = K4,500

(b) Sale Price:

Sale Price = Original Price - Discount

Sale Price = 18,000 - 4,500

Sale Price = K13,500

Answer: (a) K4,500 (b) K13,500

Example 3: Income Tax Calculation

John earns a gross salary of K120,000 per month. He pays 15% income tax. Calculate:

(a) The tax deducted

(b) His net salary (take-home pay)

Solution:

(a) Tax Deducted:

Tax = 120,000 √ó (15 √∑ 100)

Tax = 120,000 √ó 0.15

Tax = K18,000

(b) Net Salary:

Net Salary = Gross Salary - Tax

Net Salary = 120,000 - 18,000

Net Salary = K102,000

Answer: (a) K18,000 (b) K102,000

Example 4: Reverse Calculation (Finding Original Price)

After adding 16.5% VAT, a phone costs K93,200. What was the original price before VAT?

Solution:

Let original price = P

Price with VAT = P + 16.5% of P

93,200 = P + 0.165P

93,200 = 1.165P

P = 93,200 √∑ 1.165

P = K80,000

Verification: VAT = 80,000 √ó 0.165 = 13,200

Total = 80,000 + 13,200 = 93,200 ‚úì

Answer: K80,000

Example 5: Multiple Calculations

A television has a marked price of K150,000. During a sale, it gets a 20% discount, then 16.5% VAT is added to the discounted price. Calculate the final price.

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate discount:

Discount = 150,000 √ó 0.20 = K30,000

Price after discount = 150,000 - 30,000 = K120,000

Step 2: Calculate VAT on discounted price:

VAT = 120,000 √ó 0.165 = K19,800

Step 3: Final price:

Final Price = 120,000 + 19,800

Final Price = K139,800

Answer: K139,800
