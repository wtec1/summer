üìö What is the Elimination Method?

The elimination method is a technique for solving simultaneous equations where we eliminate one variable by adding or subtracting equations. This leaves us with one equation in one unknown, which we can solve easily.

Steps to Follow:

  1. Make the coefficients of one variable the same in both equations
  2. Add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable
  3. Solve for the remaining variable
  4. Substitute back to find the other variable
  5. Check your answer

Example 1

Solve: 2x + y = 8 and x - y = 1

Solution:

Step 1: Notice that y has coefficients +1 and -1

Step 2: Add both equations to eliminate y:

(2x + y) + (x - y) = 8 + 1

3x = 9

x = 3

Step 3: Substitute x = 3 into equation 2:

3 - y = 1

y = 2

Answer: x = 3, y = 2

Example 2

Solve: 3x + 2y = 12 and 2x + 2y = 10

Solution:

Step 1: Notice that y has the same coefficient (2) in both equations

Step 2: Subtract equation 2 from equation 1:

(3x + 2y) - (2x + 2y) = 12 - 10

x = 2

Step 3: Substitute x = 2 into equation 2:

2(2) + 2y = 10

4 + 2y = 10

2y = 6

y = 3

Answer: x = 2, y = 3

Example 3

Solve: 4x + 3y = 18 and 2x - y = 5

Solution:

Step 1: Multiply equation 2 by 3 to make y coefficients match:

6x - 3y = 15

Step 2: Add to equation 1:

(4x + 3y) + (6x - 3y) = 18 + 15

10x = 33

x = 3.3

Step 3: Substitute x = 3.3 into equation 2:

2(3.3) - y = 5

6.6 - y = 5

y = 1.6

Answer: x = 3.3, y = 1.6

Example 4
